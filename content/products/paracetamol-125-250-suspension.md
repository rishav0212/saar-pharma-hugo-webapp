## Mechanism of Action & Manufacturing Integrity {#mechanism}
**Clinical Mechanism of Action:**
Paracetamol exerts its analgesic and antipyretic effects primarily within the central nervous system (CNS). It increases the pain threshold by inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes—specifically COX-2 and the putative COX-3 isoform—thereby preventing the synthesis of pain-inducing prostaglandins. Its antipyretic action is achieved through direct inhibition of prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) synthesis within the hypothalamic heat-regulating center, causing vasodilation and increased peripheral blood flow, which rapidly dissipates body heat.

- **Phase Separation Prevention**: Through high-shear homogenization in our chilled manufacturing vessels, we create a perfectly uniform rheological profile. This prevents the liquid vehicle and the suspended particles from separating permanently, ensuring your brand's product remains visually appealing and clinically potent throughout its shelf life.

## Side Effects & Safety Profile {#safety}
When used at the recommended dosage, Paracetamol is exceptionally well-tolerated and side effects are rare. However, the following should be noted:
- **Adverse Effects**: Skin rashes, allergic reactions, or mild gastrointestinal disturbances have been reported very rarely.
