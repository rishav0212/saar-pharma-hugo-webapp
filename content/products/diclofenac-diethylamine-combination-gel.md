## Mechanism of Action & Formulation Integrity {#mechanism}

### How the Five Components Work Together

Diclofenac Diethylamine Combination Gel operates through three distinct but synergistic analgesic mechanisms acting simultaneously:

**NSAID-Mediated Anti-Inflammation (Diclofenac Diethylamine):** After dermal penetration, Diclofenac Diethylamine dissociates to release the active Diclofenac anion, which inhibits both COX-1 and COX-2 cyclooxygenase enzymes. This blocks the conversion of arachidonic acid to prostaglandins (PGE₂, PGI₂) and thromboxanes at the site of tissue injury. Prostaglandins are the primary mediators of peripheral sensitisation — they lower the activation threshold of nociceptors, causing hyperalgesia and allodynia. By blocking their synthesis locally, Diclofenac reduces both pain and inflammatory swelling without significant systemic NSAID exposure.

**Counter-Irritant Sensory Analgesia (Methyl Salicylate + Menthol):** Methyl Salicylate generates a warming sensation by activating TRPV1 (transient receptor potential vanilloid 1) thermoreceptors and stimulating local cutaneous circulation. This masks deeper musculoskeletal pain signals through counter-irritation — a peripheral pain gating mechanism. Menthol activates TRPM8 cold-receptor channels, producing a sustained cooling effect that desensitises the same nociceptors that are transmitting pain, compounding the analgesic effect. These two counter-irritant actions overlap in timing, creating the characteristic dual cool-then-warm sensation that patients associate with rapid gel efficacy.

**Penetration Enhancement & Anti-Inflammatory Support (Linseed Oil):** Linseed Oil (containing approximately 55–60% alpha-linolenic acid, an omega-3 fatty acid) functions both as an emollient penetration enhancer — improving the percutaneous flux of Diclofenac and Methyl Salicylate through the stratum corneum — and as a direct anti-inflammatory agent, as ALA is a substrate for anti-inflammatory eicosanoid synthesis that partially counters the arachidonic acid inflammatory cascade.

Our gel base is prepared at controlled temperature with validated pH adjustment before API incorporation, preventing hydrolytic degradation of the amine salt linkage that would reduce bioactive Diclofenac content.

Our manufacturing process uses temperature-monitored closed-vessel mixing to prevent evaporative loss of these actives during homogenization — ensuring the finished product retains its full counter-irritant potency and characteristic sensory profile.

## Storage & Handling Guidelines {#storage}

1. **Sealed Tube Storage**: Store below 30°C in a cool, dry location away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Do not freeze — freezing disrupts the gel matrix homogeneity and can cause phase separation of the Linseed Oil component, permanently altering viscosity and API distribution. Store tubes with caps tightly closed to prevent evaporation of volatile components (Menthol, Methyl Salicylate) through the tube opening.

2. **In-Use Handling**: After opening, tubes should be used within the expiry period. Replace the cap firmly after each use to minimise exposure of the product to air and light. Tubes should not be stored near heat-generating appliances or in vehicles exposed to high ambient temperatures — conditions common in the Indian summer that can accelerate degradation of the volatile actives and Diclofenac stability.

3. **Application Precautions**: Apply exclusively to intact skin. Keep away from eyes, mucous membranes, and any broken or infected skin area. Wash hands immediately after each application unless hands are the area being treated. Do not apply external heat or occlusive dressings over the treated area immediately following application, as this significantly increases percutaneous absorption and systemic NSAID exposure.

4. **Packaging Integrity**: Laminated aluminium tubes are selected for their excellent barrier properties against light, moisture, and oxygen — all of which can degrade Diclofenac Diethylamine and cause oxidative degradation of Linseed Oil's polyunsaturated fatty acid content. The internal tube lining is chemically compatible with the acidic gel components, preventing leaching or interaction with the packaging material over the 24-month shelf life.

## Side Effects & Safety Profile {#safety}

Diclofenac Diethylamine Combination Gel is generally well-tolerated when applied as directed to intact skin. The majority of adverse effects are localised to the application site.

Common (1–10 in 100 users): transient burning, stinging, or warming sensation on application (related to Methyl Salicylate and Menthol counter-irritant activity); mild skin redness and erythema; itching.

Uncommon (1–10 in 1000 users): skin dryness and peeling; localised dermatitis; prolonged erythema.

Rare: blistering of the skin; contact dermatitis; localised allergic reaction. Discontinue use and seek medical advice if any of these occur.

**Contraindications:** Known hypersensitivity to Diclofenac, aspirin, or other NSAIDs; hypersensitivity to any listed excipient; broken, infected, or eczematous skin at the application site; last trimester of pregnancy (NSAID-class contraindication). Patients with known aspirin-sensitive asthma should use with caution due to cross-reactivity risk with Methyl Salicylate.

**Use with caution in:** Patients with coagulation disorders or those on anticoagulant therapy (topical NSAIDs can affect platelet function); patients with hepatic or renal impairment; patients applying to large body surface areas for extended periods, which may increase systemic exposure.

Healthcare professionals should refer to the complete prescribing information for full safety and interaction data.
