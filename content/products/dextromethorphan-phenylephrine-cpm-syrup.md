## Mechanism of Action {#mechanism}

### Triple-Action Clinical Mechanism

**1. Dextromethorphan HBr — Central Cough Suppression**
Acts via sigma-1 receptor agonism and weak NMDA receptor antagonism in the medulla oblongata, elevating the cough threshold. Unlike codeine, it is non-narcotic and non-opioid at therapeutic doses — no risk of respiratory depression or physical dependence.

**2. Phenylephrine HCl — Nasal Decongestion**
A selective alpha-1 adrenergic agonist that causes vasoconstriction of dilated nasal submucosal vessels, reducing mucosal oedema and airway resistance. Its peripheral selectivity provides a more favourable cardiovascular safety profile compared to non-selective sympathomimetics at standard oral doses.

**3. Chlorpheniramine Maleate (CPM) — H1 Antihistamine**
Competitively and reversibly blocks peripheral H1 receptors, reducing histamine-mediated vasodilation, hypersecretion, and sneezing. Its mild anticholinergic activity further reduces nasal secretion volume, providing relief from both viral and allergic rhinorrhoea.

## Storage & Handling {#storage}

1. **Temperature**: Store sealed cartons below **30°C** in a cool, dry place. Phenylephrine HCl is susceptible to oxidative degradation at elevated temperatures — avoid heat exposure above 35°C.
2. **Light Protection**: Dextromethorphan HBr is photo-sensitive. Amber PET bottles block the UV wavelengths that drive its photodegradation; keep product in outer cartons until dispensing.
3. **In-Use**: No refrigeration required after opening. Keep tightly closed between doses and use the provided 5ml measuring cup for accurate pediatric dosing.
4. **Labeling**: All cartons must carry the Schedule H Rx warning, storage temperature, dosage table, and "Keep out of reach of children" per DCGI requirements.

## Side Effects & Safety Profile {#safety}

This combination is generally well tolerated at therapeutic doses. Product labeling must accurately communicate the following:

- **Common Adverse Effects**: Drowsiness and mild sedation (CPM's anticholinergic effect) are the most frequent. Also reported: dry mouth, mild GI discomfort, and dizziness. Patients should avoid driving or operating machinery until their individual response is known.
- **Cardiovascular Caution**: Phenylephrine can raise blood pressure at higher doses. Use with caution in hypertension, cardiovascular disease, and hyperthyroidism; prescriber review recommended for high-risk patients.
- **Contraindications**: MAOIs within 14 days, severe hypertension, coronary artery disease, narrow-angle glaucoma, urinary retention, and hypersensitivity to any component.
- **Paediatric**: Not recommended below 2 years. Use in children below 6 years only under physician guidance with appropriate weight-based dose adjustment.
